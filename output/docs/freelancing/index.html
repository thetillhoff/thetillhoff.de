Freelancing

I want to become freelancer at some point. On this page I gather some of my notes to achieve that goal.

How To Become A Freelancer

(As described by ChatGPT)
  1. Define your area of expertise
  2. Create a business plan
  3. Plan your finances
  4. Create a network
  5. Plan how to find customers
  6. Make sure you have someone at hand for contracts and legal questions
  7. Continue to learn, so there's always demand for your skill and expertise

Definition Of My Area Of Expertise

Backend, Infrastructure, DevOps, Cloud, Cloud Migrations, Project Manager, Team leadership, Culture shaping, Developer Experience Factors: - skills and experience - market demand - What makes you special? niche or generalistic? - market gaps - feedback from customers & colleagues - update regularly

My Business Plan

Goals, Target audience/group, Services/portfolio, pricing model, marketing strategy Pricing model: - check competitor pricings - pricing models: hourly, daily, weekly, long-term monthly, project-based, value-based (cost reduction, more revenue) - update regularly

Planning of my finances

Base costs like rent and equipment, insurances, taxes, social taxes, health insurance "Freiberufler" vs business

Create a network

How to reach potential customers Visibility in the industry sector (like meetups, conventions, online communities, freelancing platforms) personal brand

Plan how to find customers

Use multiple approaches together, like have your own website/portfolio, freelancing platforms, social media, contact companies, apply at "Personaldienstleister" and it-/consulting companies "Personaldienstleister" want to fill gaps in their provided services, as to consultancies Also, for short-term projects that need special skills, freelancers are better than hiring long-term for consultancies

Make sure you have someone at hand for contracts and legal questions

Make sure you can provide professional contracts Have a connection to a law advisor for legal questions

Continue to learn

Stay in high demand.
